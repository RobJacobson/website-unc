As part of this partnership of faith-based organizations\, Judea Reform is committed to supporting reforms to immigration policy that are fair and humane and reflect our mandate to welcome the stranger and treat all human beings with dignity and respect. \nCome join Judea Reform Congregation for a Shabbat Service for Immigrant Justice on Friday\, November 3 at 7:30pm. \nCome by our Selfie Photo Booth at the Oneg — we will be taking photos to share on social media to amplify the #Faith4Dream message. This is the only home they have ever known. As their friends\, allies\, and faith leaders\, it is our responsibility to do what we can to lift their voices and advocate for their safety. This is even more imperative now that President Trump has rescinded the Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals (DACA) program that kept almost 800\,000 immigrant youth protected from deportation. Congress has less than six months to act and ensure these individuals are not betrayed by the U.S. government and deported. It is critical that Congress pass a “clean” Dream Act 2017\, without enforcement provisions or other negative proposals.
