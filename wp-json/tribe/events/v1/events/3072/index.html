Featuring Mexican Human Rights Activist & Advocate for the Rights of Women in Migration, Maya Casillas Garcia<\/strong><\/p>\n

Esmeralda Maya Casillas Garcia is an advocate for the rights of women in migration. She is a native of the state of Chiapas, Mexico, where she has facilitated extensive trainings on gender equity, reproductive rights, as well as on the protection and care of migrant women and girls. Maya serves as the Coordinator and Legal Advisor for the Center for Care and Advocacy of Women’s Rights in Migration, known as “La Casita”, a project of the Mesoamerican Network working on the intersection of health, women’s rights and migration, in conjunction with a network of women organizers. As a lawyer, she has successfully pursued asylum cases for several Central American families.<\/p>\n

About the Speaking Tour. <\/strong><\/p>\n

This speaking tour will travel through the Southeast of the U.S. visiting Birmingham, AL; Atlanta, GA; Durham and Chapel Hill, NC, as part of Alianza Americas’ broader educational initiative bringing diverse experts from Latin America to the U.S.<\/em><\/p>\n

These Speaking Tours<\/a> bring voices from Mexico and Central America to different geographic areas in the U.S. The purpose of the tour is to inform and raise awareness about the violence and insecurity in Central America and the ongoing exodus of women and young people from the region, particularly the countries of the Northern Triangle: Honduras, El Salvador, and Guatemala.<\/em><\/p>\n
