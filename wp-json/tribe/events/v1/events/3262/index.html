 <\/p>\n

On September 28, immigrant families and their supporters will gather in Greensboro, Winston-Salem & Durham to highlight the terrible dilemma that led three North Carolina families to seek refuge in three different houses of worship this summer, to avoid the heartless demands of ICE agents.<\/p>\n

We’ll gather to remember and memorialize some of the dozens of people who did _not_ find refuge, and lost their lives after being deported. ICE detentions have surged over 70% this year so far, and ICE continues to choose which people to deport seemingly at random.<\/p>\n

North Carolina now has 20% of all active sanctuary cases nationally, the most of any state. How many more will need to make this impossible choice this year in our state?<\/span><\/p>\n

How many more families will open the mail suddenly to discover that their grandmother or mother’s case has been reopened by ICE, and that she is being ordered to leave the country?<\/p>\n

How many children will come home from school to find a parent missing?<\/p>\n

What are our elected representatives planning to do to end this crisis, and protect our undocumented family members and neighbors?<\/p>\n
