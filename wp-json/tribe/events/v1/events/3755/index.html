\nEarlier this year, the Alamance County Sheriff’s office announced their interest to re-enter into a 287 (g) partnership with Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE). This surprised many due to devastation the program caused during its first implementation in the county which culminated with a Department of Justice led investigation for suspicion of racial discrimination. Join us for community presentation and discussion about the 287 (g) program and its impact in our immigrant and broader community.<\/p>\n

-“The 287(g) program, one of ICE’s top partnership initiatives, allows a state or local law enforcement entity to enter into a partnership with ICE, under a joint Memorandum of Agreement (MOA),\u00a0in order to receive delegated authority for immigration enforcement within their jurisdictions.”
