In early October 2016 a banner in rural Atkinson, NC read, “We must kill the white trash who hire illegal aliens,\u201d purporting to cite\u00a0the Bible as support.<\/p>\n

Uniting NC strives to make North Carolina feel like home for everyone, including immigrants and refugees. This is why we created a billboard, shown above, that is located 10 miles away from the small town.<\/p>\n

Jennie Belle with the North Carolina Council of Churches was particularly concerned with the Atkinson sign\u2019s use of scripture to support an apparent call to violence. \u201cAs members of the faith community, we are called to love our neighbor \u2014 all of our neighbors.\u201d<\/p>\n

Belle believed this message was particularly needed now. \u201cIn these difficult times when hateful rhetoric is being used daily by people on both sides of conflicting issues, we must come together to build a country that models inclusion and understanding \u2014 not just on issues of immigration, but on race relations in our country overall. We must remember that our faith calls us to love our neighbor, welcome the stranger, and stand in solidarity with the vulnerable.\u201d<\/p>\n

The new billboard is located on U.S. 117 E in Burgaw, just west of U.S. interstate 40.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
