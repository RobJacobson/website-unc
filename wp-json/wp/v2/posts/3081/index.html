On Saturday Durham’s Central Park came to life\u00a0with diversity and unity as local refugee resettlement agencies CWS\u00a0and World Relief sponsored the area’s first Refugee Day.\u00a0Uniting NC was one of a number of nonprofit organizations that joined in support of local refugee families. In addition to sharing information about our work, staff also sold “NC is home” shirts (with ‘home’ in 8 different languages) as a joint fundraiser for CWS, World Relief and Uniting NC. We also ran into many families who were proud to say they had a\u00a0“Welcome to Durham Y’all<\/a>” or “Welcome to Raleigh Y’all<\/a>” sign planted in their yard. Families laughed and played while listening to music and enjoying\u00a0delicious food — a wonderful celebration of new neighbors, new opportunities and all that we can learn from one another.<\/p>\n
