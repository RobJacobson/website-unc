At Chobani, Now It\u2019s Not Just the Yogurt That\u2019s Rich<\/h1>\n
\n
\n
\n

NEW BERLIN, N.Y. \u2014 The 2,000 full-time employees of the yogurt company Chobani were handed quite the surprise on Tuesday: an ownership stake that could make some of them millionaires.<\/p>\n

Hamdi Ulukaya, the Turkish immigrant who founded Chobani in 2005, told workers at the company\u2019s plant here in upstate New York that he would be giving them shares worth up to 10 percent of the company when it goes public or is sold.<\/p>\n

The goal, he said, is to pass along the wealth they have helped build in the decade since the company started. Chobani is now widely considered to be worth several billion dollars.<\/p>\n

\u201cI\u2019ve built something I never thought would be such a success, but I cannot think of Chobani being built without all these people,\u201d Mr. Ulukaya said in an interview in his Manhattan office that was granted on the condition that no details of the program would be disclosed before the announcement.<\/p>\n

\u201cNow they\u2019ll be working to build the company even more and building their future at the same time,\u201d he said.<\/p>\n

Chobani employees received the news on Tuesday morning. Each worker received a white packet; inside was information about how many Chobani shares they were given. The number of shares given to each person is based on tenure, so the longer an employee has been at the company, the bigger the stake.<\/p><\/div>\n<\/div>\n

\n
\n

Two years ago, when Chobani\u00a0received a loan<\/a>\u00a0from TPG Capital, a\u00a0private equity<\/a>\u00a0firm, the company\u2019s value was estimated at $3 billion to $5 billion. At the $3 billion valuation, the average employee payout would be $150,000. The earliest employees, though, will most likely be given many more shares, possibly worth over $1 million.<\/p>\n

Rich Lake, lead project manager, was one of the original group of five employees Mr. Ulukaya hired for the plant in New Berlin. Mr. Lake said on Tuesday that he did not expect Chobani shares to change his life much. \u201cI\u2019m not one for living outside my means,\u201d he said.<\/p>\n

Rather, he said, the shares are an acknowledgment of what he and the other employees have put into Chobani.<\/p>\n

\u201cIt\u2019s better than a bonus or a raise,\u201d Mr. Lake said. \u201cIt\u2019s the best thing because you\u2019re getting a piece of this thing you helped build.\u201d<\/p>\n
