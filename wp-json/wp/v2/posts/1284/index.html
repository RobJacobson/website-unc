Guest post from Uniting NC assistant Francisco Chavez.<\/em><\/p>\n

Most people understand by now that \u201creality television\u201d is far from realistic. But a new show on the Lifetime network \u2014 which trains the camera on a group of people who have roots in former Soviet countries \u2014 has stirred controversy<\/a> over whether these shows promote prejudice and cultural stereotypes.<\/p>\n

\u201cRussian Dolls\u201d<\/a> follows eight first- and second-generation immigrants living in New York\u2019s Brighton Beach.\u00a0 The promotional video portrays marching soldiers and images of Matryoshka dolls; followed by beautiful women and brawls at nightclubs.<\/p>\n

The show\u2019s women flounce around in short dresses and wave knives during arguments, and one breaks up with a Latino boyfriend simply because he\u2019s not Russian. The men make vulgar comments about women.<\/p>\n

The cast says it\u2019s all meant in the spirit of fun, and some fans with Russian roots say the show is an accurate depiction of one aspect of Russian culture \u2014 just like the popular \u201cJersey Shore\u201d shows only one small slice of the culture of New Jersey.<\/p>\n

Others are outraged at the misrepresentation of their culture and offended that the show makes all Russians look like \u201cprostitutes and criminals.\u201d<\/p>\n

These immigrants are not the first minority group to be exploited and stereotyped for the sake of “reality” television. The New York times had this take<\/a>:<\/p>\n

Some Russian-Americans may be offended by \u201cRussian Dolls,\u201d and they can get in line behind the Italian-Americans still fuming over \u201cJersey Shore\u201d or African-Americans appalled by \u201cThe Real Housewives of Atlanta.\u201d Stereotypes are to reality shows what nuclear fusion is to the hydrogen bomb: you simply can\u2019t have one without the other.<\/p><\/blockquote>\n

For us at Uniting NC, the show is just another reminder of how much work we have to do. With these kinds of\u00a0 extreme images of immigrants being promoted in the media and on TV, we must work even harder to show people the true picture.<\/p>\n

Immigrants cannot be shoe-horned into stereotypes. They are not all good or all bad. They are just people, and should be treated as individuals.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
