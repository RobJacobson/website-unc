Last week, Uniting NC held a volunteer training that brought together about 20 people to talk about how we can spread this movement to make North Carolina a welcoming and inclusive place. The enthusiasm in the room was truly inspiring, and many didn’t want to wait to get started. On Saturday, several new volunteers helped us man a table at Ritmo Latino, a yearly festival in Cary that celebrates Latin culture. Below is a guest post from Renee Elder, one of those who attended the training and helped out at the festival.<\/em><\/p>\n

<\/a><\/p>\n

Beautiful weather, colorful\u00a0costumes\u00a0and intriguing\u00a0Latin rhythms were all on display Saturday, May 19,\u00a0at the Ritmo Latino festival in downtown Cary.<\/p>\n

Between Zumba lessons and sampling\u00a0the\u00a0tasty Latin dishes, Uniting NC volunteers and board members took the opportunity to talk with individuals one-on-one about\u00a0the group’s welcoming mission and hear inspiring family\u00a0immigration stories\u00a0from several guests.<\/p>\n

With more than a dozen nonprofit organizations represented,\u00a0we also did some cross-pollinating, finding\u00a0threads that\u00a0can be woven together\u00a0in support of each other’s efforts.<\/p>\n

The Ritmo Latino (or Latin Rhythm) festival\u00a0has taken place in Cary for the past several years, but was moved from August to May this year to avoid the heat of late summer. The\u00a0decision was rewarded with a perfect 78-degree day and throngs of enthusiastic people with ties to countries around the world.<\/p>\n

Woven into the festive atmosphere was\u00a0evidence that North Carolina and\u00a0the Triangle is\u00a0an amazingly\u00a0diverse community — no longer\u00a0confined to a\u00a0single or exclusive cultural box.\u00a0 And that is certainly\u00a0algo para celebrar!<\/p>\n
