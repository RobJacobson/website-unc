This fall we started a new program called Code the Dream<\/a>. Let me tell you why we did, and why it is so exciting.<\/p>\n

The Challenge<\/strong><\/p>\n

\n
\n
\n
\n

Tech startups now embody the American Dream. Young people dream of getting a job with Google or starting the next Twitter. The creativity and entrepreneurial spirit behind these new technologies represent the best of our country. But a large and growing part of America is being left out.<\/p>\n

\n
\n
\n
\n

Silicon Valley Hasn\u2019t Solved it Yet…<\/strong><\/p>\n

In Silicon Valley and across the country respected companies like Google are acknowledging their striking lack of diversity. And by failing to encourage smart young minds from all diverse communities to enter our most dynamic sector, we put the American Dream at risk for all of us.<\/p>\n

We Need the Best and the Brightest<\/strong><\/p>\n

We often hear that we don’t have enough qualified people to fill the needs of our booming tech economy, that\u00a0we need more visas to allow in the best and the brightest from around the world. And maybe\u00a0we do. But many of the\u00a0best and the brightest are already here and haven’t been exposed to tech and given a real chance.<\/p>\n

For instance, many talented immigrant youth with Deferred Action status graduate near the top of their high school class, they take AP classes, they have all the tools to succeed, but then they remain ineligible for in-state tuition or student loans.\u00a0Without a chance to go to college, many are left working low-income jobs to support themselves and their families. And all of us lose out on their talents.<\/p>\n

Code the Dream<\/strong><\/p>\n

Code the Dream provides a gateway to the tech industry for minority and immigrant youth. In the first month, students learn to code their first app. They learn from mentors in the tech industry, meet startup founders and get a sense for how they could make their own dreams a reality \u2014 by working on real world projects to benefit the community and learning what it means to be a professional software developer or one day start their own business.<\/p>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n

\n
\n
\n
\n

Together we can lead the way \u2014 growing the type of vibrant economy that can only flourish with the inclusion of all our diverse communities. Code the Dream is an opportunity for all of us.<\/p>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n

To learn more about how it works, visit www.codethedream.org<\/a>.<\/strong><\/p>\n
