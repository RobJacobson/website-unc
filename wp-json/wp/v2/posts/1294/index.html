This Wednesday is the United Nations’ International Day of Peace<\/a>. Across North Carolina and the world, people will be working to add peace to the world on this day.<\/p>\n

Of course, many of us do this work all the time. At Uniting NC, we believe our work to build understanding and empathy between people of all backgrounds and cultures is a crucial step toward building a peaceful community and a peaceful world. When we see others as human beings like ourselves, no matter how foreign they may seem at first glance, we are less inclined to feel threatened or ready to fight.<\/p>\n

But this Wednesday, we’re asking you to think about one small extra thing you can do to celebrate peace. The nonprofit Cultural Enrichment Services<\/a> has come up with a handy list of ways you can participate in this day, and here are a few of their suggestions for peaceful activities:<\/p>\n

    \n
  • Write a poem or essay, take a photograph, or create a video that speaks to you about peace. Share it with your friends or post it online.<\/li>\n
  • Watch a peaceful film.<\/a><\/li>\n
  • Plant a rock for peace<\/a>.<\/li>\n
  • Blog about Peace Day.<\/li>\n
  • Host a multi-cultural dinner for peace.<\/li>\n
  • Talk to your children about what makes them feel peaceful.<\/li>\n
  • Create peaceful art: origami doves, pinwheels for peace<\/a>, or whatever you like. (If it’s made by children, it can be entered in the Peace Pals art exhibition<\/a>.)<\/li>\n<\/ul>\n

    If you don’t have time for any of this, just hug your family, say a kind word to a stranger, do a good deed for a friend. Every little bit of peace and kindness helps.<\/p>\n

    We would love to hear your ideas for ways that individuals can contribute to a peaceful world.<\/p>\n

    And finally, a quote from Pema Chodron that gives me courage in my work: “It\u2019s important to remember, when we\u2019re out there non-aggressively working for reform, that, even if our particular issue doesn\u2019t get resolved, we are adding peace to the world.”<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
