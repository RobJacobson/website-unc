At one of Uniting NC\u2019s recent events, a woman talked about her conversion to Islam. She said that, once she began wearing a hijab to cover her head, she felt that people no longer saw her. Instead, they saw only her difference. She no longer had friendly conversations in shops or exchanged smiles on the sidewalk. People kept their distance. She felt a loneliness that she had never before experienced.<\/p>\n

As soon as I heard her words, I knew that I was one of the offenders. How many times had I seen a woman in a hijab \u2014 in the gym, in line at a store \u2014 and avoided her eyes? Had I ever made a friendly offhand comment to a woman in a hijab, the way I frequently do with people who look more like me? In that moment, I realized that, unconsciously, I had decided that those women were simply too different, that I could never find anything in common with them. Without even thinking about it, I had dehumanized them.<\/p>\n

My work with Uniting NC has opened my eyes. I now see that Muslim women, and men, are not mysterious or strange \u2014 and that many of them like to laugh and chat as much as I do. Now, we want more people to get to know their Muslim neighbors. That is why we are teaming up with the Islamic Association of Raleigh. Several months ago, the association\u2019s members came to us asking how they could help, and how we could work together to show North Carolina that Muslim Americans love this state and want to be fully participating members of their communities.<\/p>\n

We held a joint film screening earlier this spring, and next, we plan to erect a billboard highlighting the humanity of Muslim people. This billboard will be a statement about the kind of place North Carolina and the Triangle want to be. It will show that we are not content to give into our stereotypes and assumptions, that we believe in giving everyone a fair chance \u2014 regardless of their religion, nationality or culture.<\/p>\n

<\/a><\/p>\n

If this is a message you support, will you consider giving even a small donation to make this billboard possible?<\/strong> Billboards in the Triangle cost thousands of dollars a month. But if we all work together, we can send a message that reflects our core values as Americans and as North Carolinians. And if you make your donation now, we will be able to double your money.<\/strong> Thanks to a grant from the Paul Green Foundation Fund of Triangle Community Foundation, we will be able to match the first $1000 in donations.<\/p>\n
