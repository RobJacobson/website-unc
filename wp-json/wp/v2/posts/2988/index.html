<\/a>Co-creation:<\/strong> I hear a voice of truth above the clamor of the dominant culture, a voice that asks me to question my culture\u2019s assumptions and beliefs. As a servant leader, I align my life with this truth and engage with others to be co-creative instruments of justice and peace.\u00a0(Co-creation is one of the five core values of the Johnson Service Corps where I am a corps member.)<\/h6>\n

Recently I was fortunate to attend Welcoming America\u2019s conference, Welcoming Initiative. I have worked\u00a0seven months with Uniting NC striving\u00a0to make North Carolina feel like home to everyone especially immigrants and refugees.\u00a0While attending the Welcoming Initiative, I saw co-creation\u00a0in action. I met other young professionals from around the country with the same goal to make the United States welcoming to our new neighbors.<\/p>\n

One takeaway from the conference was to shift the language identifying\u00a0immigrants and refugees. Instead of using a general term, why not use \u2018new neighbor?\u2019 I think this is a beautiful change because it shifts the identity of the individual from\u00a0focusing on their past and instead shows warmth and a\u00a0hopeful sign of welcome into their new community. Moving forward, I am excited to welcome new neighbors and continue this important work.<\/p>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n<\/div>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
