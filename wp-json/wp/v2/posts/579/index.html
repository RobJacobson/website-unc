Today, a federal appeals court struck down<\/a> a Pennsylvania town’s ordinances prohibiting businesses and landlords from hiring or renting to illegal immigrants. The judges said the controversial ordinances, passed by the Hazelton city council in 2006, usurped federal authority to enforce immigration law.<\/p>\n

Similar ordinances that have attempted to crack down on immigration at the local level have been passed around the country, most notably in Arizona. Courts have blocked them all, and none has yet been enforced.<\/p>\n

All these laws, and the resulting court battles, have been driven by the idea that we can reverse the tide of immigration \u2014 that if we pass enough unfriendly laws, our communities will go back to being English-only zones. But the Hazelton decision shows us, once again, that immigrants are here to stay.<\/p>\n

So, let’s accept reality.<\/p>\n

It’s time to end the legal fights and the town hall brawls and think about how we can better live together. It’s time to figure out how every resident of a community, regardless of\u00a0nationality, can become a contributing member. It’s time we all start looking at each other as human beings.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
