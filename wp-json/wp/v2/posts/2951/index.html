As tensions rise\u00a0within immigrant and refugee communities across the country, the cities of Raleigh and Durham are demonstrating a warm welcome. If you’ve driven through downtown Raleigh or walked amongst some of the local businesses in Durham, you’ll notice a unique sign. The locally known sign that says “Welcome to Raleigh, Y’all” and “Welcome to Durham, Y’all” has welcome written in 16 languages.<\/p>\n

The sign was created by Grayson Haver Currin\u00a0and Tina Haver Currin, who run the no<\/a>n-profit Come Out & Show Them<\/a>. As of now,\u00a03,000 stickers and signs have been distributed between these two cities.<\/p>\n

All of the proceeds that are collected from this campaign will help aid refugees and immigrants through the\u00a0U.S. Committee for Refugees, Immigrants (USCRI NC) and Uniting NC.<\/p>\n

The campaign is still expanding and has been featured on \u00a0WUNC<\/a>\u00a0and in the\u00a0The News & Observer<\/a>. We hope you join us in welcoming our new neighbors with this sign!<\/p>\n
