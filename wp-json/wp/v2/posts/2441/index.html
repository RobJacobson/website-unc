For many\u00a0students in\u00a0Uniting NC’s Code the Dream<\/a>\u00a0program, part of their dream includes one day running\u00a0their own business. Some already have ideas in mind\u00a0and are ready to get to work,\u00a0but they aren’t sure where to start.<\/p>\n

Immigrants to the United States have a long history of entrepreneurship. Across the country immigrants today\u00a0are starting\u00a0businesses at twice the rate as the rest of us. But businesses started by lower-income entrepreneurs, whether immigrants or not, often lack access to technologies that can make it easier to manage and grow a business. Likewise, entrepreneurs from lower-income communities have thus far been much less likely to start the type of innovative software or tech companies that often have the highest potential for dramatic growth.<\/p>\n
