This past week, Uniting NC had the opportunity to be part of a fantastic project. Charlotte artist Edwin Gil, a native of Colombia, is touring the state creating what he calls the Flag of Hope<\/a>. The flag is actually a massive canvas covered in hand prints made by people all over North Carolina. Atop their hand prints, each person has written their name and the city where they were born. When it is finished, the prints will form the image of the sun on the horizon, green earth below and blue sky above. It will be displayed in downtown Charlotte.<\/p>\n

Aside from being beautiful, the flag celebrates the rich and diverse fabric of our state. It is, in many ways, a visual representation of the message that Uniting NC is working to spread across North Carolina. It shows us that, while we come from many places, we are now all part of the same picture. Every hand helps create the tableau, but none could do it alone. The hand print is also a potent symbol of how much we have in common. No matter where we come from, how different our lives or our backgrounds may be, we all make the same mark on the canvas. Our hand prints look so much more alike than different.<\/p>\n
