Today\u2019s N&O includes a great story<\/a> about Uniting NC\u2019s most recent volunteer day.<\/p>\n

Last<\/a> Saturday we continued our series of service events\u00a0 bringing together immigrants and non-immigrants to give back to their community. And again the response was wonderful. This time we had over 30 volunteers from 10 different countries all working together on a community farm that provides fresh produce to the Food Bank of Eastern and Central North Carolina.<\/p>\n

We sometimes hear cynics tell us that people are coming to the U.S. looking for handouts. They emphasize costs without considering contributions. But have they ever met the people they\u2019re talking about? Anyone with us on the farm on Saturday would have seen a hard-working group of volunteers giving up their Saturday to pick blueberries; plant sweet potatoes, melons, tomatoes and peppers; weed row after row of new crops; and generally do whatever was needed. And this generosity is by no means limited to a Uniting NC event. Why is that? Because it\u2019s that same hard-working, giving nature that brings immigrants in the first place \u2014 to provide for their families and support those who stayed behind.<\/p>\n

We\u2019ve had volunteers who were native North Carolinians and \u2018Yankee\u2019 transplants; young people who came here to study and are now successful professionals; and many recent immigrants and refugees who are still struggling to get by. It\u2019s amazing how eager they are to help, particularly those still working hard themselves to reach the American Dream.\u00a0 For those who think of being helpful and giving as core American attributes, then these volunteers are already as American as can be.<\/p>\n

Check out more pictures on our Facebook page<\/a> and ‘Like’ our work.<\/p>\n
