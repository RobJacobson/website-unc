Check out this fascinating interview<\/a> with a neuroscientist who has found that bilingualism sharpens the mind and delays the onset of Alzheimer’s symptoms.<\/p>\n

Dr. Ellen Bialystok has been researching the cognitive effects of speaking two languages for nearly 40 years. She has found that it trains the mind to better sort through information and focus on relevant facts. Bilingual children did better on tests, and bilingual adults were better at multi-tasking.<\/p>\n

She also looked at the medical records of 400 Alzheimer’s patients and found that, for the bilingual ones, symptoms started an average of five or six years later.<\/p>\n

“Bilingualism is good for you,” Bialystok says. “It makes brains stronger. It is brain exercise.”<\/p>\n

At Uniting NC, we think that understanding other cultures and languages is important for creating safe and prosperous communities where everyone can fulfill their potential.
