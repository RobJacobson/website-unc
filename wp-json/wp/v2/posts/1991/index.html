Yesterday we ‘kicked’ off National Welcoming Week<\/a> with a bang! Check out the stories about it in the N&O<\/a> and Durham Herald Sun<\/a>.<\/p>\n

<\/div>\n


<\/p>\n

We called it “Kicking It Together,” and the idea was to bring together the Triangle’s diverse communities to play the one sport that the world seems to be able to agree on – soccer. Or is that f\u00fatbol or football?<\/p>\n

Unofficially we are now calling it “the greatest international, multicultural soccer extravaganza ever known on planet Earth.” And that’s only a slight exaggeration. Those of us born and raised around here were joined by folks from Somalia, Sudan, Eritrea, Burma, Argentina, Denmark, Mexico, Guatemala, China, the Central African Republic, Iraq, England, and the Congo. Some have lived here for years, but others – especially recent refugees resettled by CWS<\/a> – have only been here for a matter of weeks. All together we had more than 80 people from around the Triangle join us on a cool, bright sunny morning.<\/p>\n

Of course what’s truly amazing is that soccer games like this spring up on their own on fields across North Carolina every weekend. Maybe not with a dozen countries represented, but certainly with three or four. The mix of languages, the different backgrounds and the laughter and camaraderie that you can find on any given Saturday are inspiring.<\/p>\n

<\/p>\n

Yesterday’s event however was particularly special because so many of the recent immigrants and refugees hadn’t had this opportunity since coming to the United States. Many don’t yet have cars, so CWS<\/a> and Uniting NC volunteers pitched in to give rides to the field. Many also lacked cleats to play in. But soccer.com<\/a>, based right here in Hillsborough, came through with cleats\u00a0 – and socks, balls, pop-up goals and more. There were big smiles all around! All our National Welcoming Week activities are also made possible by generous support from BB&T<\/a>. Thank you so much to our sponsors and volunteers.<\/p>\n

All photos by Ricky Leung. For more pictures, and to learn about upcoming events, check out our Facebook page<\/a>!<\/p>\n
