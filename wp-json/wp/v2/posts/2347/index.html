<\/a>Our first group of Code the Dream students have worked incredibly\u00a0hard — both in the classroom and putting what they are learning into practice on their own — and exceeded everyone’s expectations. From having little or no programing experience, all have now coded their first apps! The\u00a0entire team of students and mentors have been amazing and are looking forward to keeping up the momentum.<\/p>\n

Then earlier this month, a group of Code the Dream students showed their creativity and entrepreneurial spirit when they participated in Startup Weekend<\/a>. Their idea was to create\u00a0an app to help immigrant students looking to go to college. After an intense weekend of brainstorming, coding and learning along the way, Keny made the final pitch. When the crowd voting was done, Code the Dream’s startup had come in second out of the 12 final pitches, by one vote. And that’s when Keny told us he’d voted for the other team! According to Keny, there is apparently an unwritten rule against voting for your own idea! Well, he’s going to be hearing about that for a while.<\/p>\n
